## What "Modern" Actually Means

I'm not talking about slapping a WordPress template together and calling it a day. A modern website in 2026 means:

### Fast Load Times

Google has made it crystal clear: **page speed is a ranking factor.** If your site takes more than 3 seconds to load, over half your visitors will leave before they see anything. The average mobile user in 2026 expects a page to load in under 2 seconds.

Most old websites are bloated with unoptimized images, outdated plugins, and slow hosting. A modern site built on a framework like Next.js can load in under a second.

### Mobile-First Design

**Over 60% of all web traffic now comes from mobile devices.** In a tourism-heavy area like Stuart, that number is probably higher — visitors are browsing on their phones while sitting at the dock or driving down A1A.

If your site doesn't look and work perfectly on a phone screen, you're turning away the majority of your potential customers.

### SEO That Actually Works

Having a website is step one. Having a website that ranks on Google is what actually drives business. That means:

- **Proper page titles and meta descriptions** targeting keywords people actually search for
- **Fast load times** (Google rewards speed)
- **Mobile responsiveness** (Google uses mobile-first indexing)
- **Local SEO optimization** — your Google Business Profile, consistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone) across the web, and location-specific content
- **Fresh content** — a blog or news section that shows Google your site is active

### Clear Calls-to-Action

Every page on your website should make it dead obvious what the visitor should do next. Call you. Book online. Request a quote. Fill out a form. If someone has to hunt around to figure out how to contact you, they won't bother.

### SSL Security

That little padlock icon in the browser bar? It means your site has an SSL certificate. Without it, Google literally warns visitors that your site is "Not Secure." That alone will tank your credibility and your rankings.
